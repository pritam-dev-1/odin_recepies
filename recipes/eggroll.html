Eggroll


Egg rolls are crispy, deep-fried American Chinese appetizers filled with shredded cabbage, pork, and vegetables, typically served with dipping sauces like soy or sweet chili. Developed in early 20th-century New York, they differ from spring rolls by having a thicker wheat wrapper. They are best fried at for a crunchy texture.

Depending on where you are in the world, an egg roll can mean two very different things: the crispy, deep-fried Chinese-American appetizer or the popular Indian street-style wrap (Kolkata Egg Roll)


Now let see how to make Kolkata Egg Roll


    Kolkata-Style Egg Roll

    This is a flaky paratha (flatbread) layered with a fried egg and filled with a zesty onion-cucumber salad.

  • Prep time: 15 minutes

  • Cook time: 15 minutes

  • Serves: 2 people

    • Ingredients:

  • Wrap: 2 flaky parathas (store-bought or homemade) and 2–4 eggs.

  • Salad Filling: Sliced red onions, julienned cucumber, chopped green chilies, and lime juice.

  • Seasoning: Chaat masala, black salt, and tomato ketchup.

    • Instructions:

  • Prepare Salad: Mix onions, cucumber, and chilies with lime juice. Set aside to "pickle" slightly.

  • Fry Paratha & Egg: Heat a skillet with a little oil. Cook the paratha until golden. Pour a beaten egg (seasoned with salt) onto the skillet and immediately press the paratha onto the wet egg. Once the egg is set, flip and cook for a few more seconds.

  • Assemble: Place the paratha egg-side up. Add a row of the salad mixture, a sprinkle of chaat masala, and a drizzle of ketchup. Roll it up tightly and wrap the bottom in parchment paper.
