How to make Momo

Make delicious homemade momos by preparing a dough of flour, salt, and water, paired with a finely chopped vegetable
or chicken filling sautéed with garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and spices. Steam them for 10–15 minutes until tender, and
serve hot with a spicy chili-garlic chutney for an authentic street-style experience

Steps of making Momos

  1. Prepare the Dough

    2. The wrapper should be thin but strong enough to hold the filling without tearing.

    • Ingredients: 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida), 1 tbsp oil, and a pinch of salt.

    • Kneading: Gradually add warm water and knead until you have a smooth, firm, and pliable dough.

    • Resting : Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes to allow the gluten to relax.

  2. Prepare the Filling

    3. You can choose between vegetarian or meat fillings. Finely chopping ingredients is crucial for a good texture

    • Veg Option: Sauté finely chopped cabbage, carrots, onions, and spring onions with ginger-garlic paste,
      soy sauce, black pepper, and a little oil.

    • Chicken/Meat Option: Mix minced meat (chicken, pork, or buff) with finely chopped onions, ginger,
      garlic, cilantro, and a dash of soy sauce.

    • Pro Tip: Adding a tablespoon of butter or ghee to the filling keeps it juicy after steaming.

  3. Shape the Momos


    • Roll: Divide the rested dough into small balls. Roll each into a thin circle about 3–4 inches in diameter.

    • Tip: Aim to keep the edges thinner than the centre so they pleat easily without becoming too doughy at the top.

    • Pleat: Place 1 tablespoon of filling in the centre. Bring the edges together and pinch them into pleats to seal either
      in a round "pouch" or "half-moon" shape.

  4. Steaming

    • Grease: Lightly oil your steamer tray to prevent sticking.

    • Steam: Arrange momos with space between them. Steam for 5–7 minutes for vegetable momos or 10–12 minutes
      for meat momos.

    • Check: They are done when the wrapper looks glossy, slightly transparent, and is no longer sticky to the touch.

  5. Signature Momo Chutney

    6. A classic spicy red chutney is made by blending roasted or boiled tomatoes, dry red chillies, garlic, and roasted sesame seeds.
