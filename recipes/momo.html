How to make Momo

Make delicious homemade momos by preparing a dough of flour, salt, and water, paired with a finely chopped vegetable or chicken filling sautéed with garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and spices. Steam them for 10–15 minutes until tender, and serve hot with a spicy chili-garlic chutney for an authentic street-style experience

Steps of making Momos

  • Ingredients For Momos
    1. Flour: All purpose flour or maida forms the base of the dough. You can use whole wheat flour but the wrapper will be really dense and chewy. All purpose flour or maida also adds more gluten and makes the wrapper easier to roll and pleat.
    2. Oil: Helps make the dough moist and pliable. I have used sunflower oil, but any neutral-flavored oil like canola or rice bran will also work.
    3. Lukewarm water: This is important to make the dough soft, pliable, and easy to work with. Hot water can make the dough tough and stringy, while cold water can make the wrappers dense, chewy and difficult to roll out.
    4. Salt: For seasoning
    5. Corn flour: For dusting (if needed)
