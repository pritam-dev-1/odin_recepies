Biriyani Recipe


Making a great chicken biryani is all about the layers and the marination. While there are many regional styles, this classic Pakki Dum Biryani method—where the meat and rice are partially cooked before being steamed together—is a foolproof way to get restaurant-quality results at home.

Steps of making Chicken Biriyani

  • Step-by-Step Instructions

    1. Marinate the Chicken In a large bowl, mix the yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, ground spices, lemon juice, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well. Marinate for at least 1 hour, or ideally overnight in the fridge for the best flavor.

    2. Prepare the Fried Onions (Birista) Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. Fry the sliced onions on medium heat, stirring frequently, until they turn uniformly golden brown and crispy. Remove half for garnish and leave the rest in the pot.

    3. Cook the Chicken Curry Add the whole spices to the remaining onions and oil. Sauté for a minute, then add the marinated chicken. Cook on medium heat for 10–15 minutes until the chicken is about 80-90% cooked and the gravy has thickened into a rich masala.

  • Layer and "Dum" (Steam)

    1. Spread the chicken evenly at the bottom of the pot.

    2. Layer the parboiled rice over the chicken.

    3. Top with remaining fried onions, chopped mint, coriander, a drizzle of ghee, and saffron milk (saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk) for color and aroma.

  • Rest and Serve
